After separate the hotdogs from Italian sausage, the analysis of variance (ANOVA) was used to determine whether the plant-based hotdogs should be compared with individual component of meat-based hotdogs. From the ANOVA result, the only attribute that is significantly different at 95% confidence interval for meat-based hotdogs is springiness. The Tukeys post hoc test was then conducted to determine how each component is different. Only the Turkey-Chicken-Pork meat blended and Beef is significantly different with each other; however, it is narrowly different. Thus, it was determined that plant-based hotdogs should be compared with overall meat-based hotdogs.

As for the Italian sausages, the only attribute that is not significantly different is adhesiveness. * Hardness The soy protein-based sausages hardness is not significantly different from meat sausages while vital wheat gluten-based sausages are not significantly different from pea protein and soy protein sausages. Additionally, while soy protein and pea protein sausages are significantly different in hardness with each other, the result is almost on the fence. It can be inferred from this post-hoc test that soy protein is appropriate for accurately imitating meat sausages in hardness.

  • Resilience For resilience, pea protein, soy protein, and vital wheat gluten sausages are not significantly different from conventional meat sausages while soy protein and pea protein are significantly different from each other. The vital wheat gluten is significantly different from pea protein; however, it is not significantly different from soy protein sausages. To imitate the accurate resilience of meat Italian sausages, the ratio of plant-based proteins should be test.

  • Cohesiveness Pea protein and soy protein-based sausages cohesiveness is not significantly different from meat sausages, on the other hand, vital wheat glutens cohesiveness is. While pea protein and soy protein are not different from meat sausages, they are also not significantly different from each other. Vital wheat gluten is not significantly different from soy protein but is with pea protein. This can be concluded that, in cohesiveness, pea protein and soy protein are the best option to imitate conventional Italian sausages.

  • Springiness Pea protein is significantly different from meat-based sausages in springiness while soy protein and vital wheat gluten are not different. From this post-hoc test, soy protein and vital wheat gluten are the best option for accurately imitating springiness of meat-based sausages.

  • Gumminess Pea protein sausages are the only test sausages that are significantly different from meat sausages. This can be inferred that, in gumminess, soy protein and vital wheat gluten can be used to accurately imitate conventional meat sausages.

  • Chewiness Pea protein sausages are the only test sausages that are significantly different from meat sausages similar to gumminess. This can be inferred that, in chewiness, soy protein and vital wheat gluten can be used to accurately imitate conventional meat sausages.

In conclusion based on the Anova test, to make the accurate imitation of conventional meat sausages, the best protein to use as a main component of Italian sausages is soy protein as it can accurately imitate the all texture attributes of meat-based sausages while the vital wheat gluten can only imitate the resilience, springiness, gumminess, and chewiness of the conventional sausages. Additionally, only texture attributes of soy protein that similar to meat sausages are resilience and cohesiveness.

Principal component analysis

These are visualization of every texture attributes, i.e.Hardness, adhesiveness, resilience, cohesiveness, springiness, gumminess, and chewiness, and how every hotdogs and sausages are influenced by those attributes, and how are they similar or different to each other, which can be used to determine the best commercially used plant protein to imitate the meat hotdogs and sausages.

Principal component analysis of hotdogs

PCA_HD <- prcomp(HD[,c(5:11)], center = TRUE, scale. = TRUE)
ggbiplot(PCA_HD, ellipse = TRUE, labels = HD$Product, groups = HD$Component)

  • Loading plot explanation
    • Adhesiveness and cohesion strongly influenced PC2
    • Gumminess and chewiness are closely related to each other and influenced PC1 (near vector)
    • Hardness, gumminess and chewiness are related to each other (near vector)
    • Resilience and springiness are likely to be related to each other (near vector)
    • Adhesiveness and springiness are not likely to be related to each other (90 degree angle)
  • PCA explanation
    • Vital wheat gluten and soy protein are overlapping with meat hotdogs
    • Good to use Vital wheat gluten and soy protein for making plant-based hotdogs

Identify limitations of visualizations used

  • The ratio used to make the best imitation could not be answered with the PCA visualizations. This require more research and data
  • For the PCA and Tukeys plots, it is required to have background knowledge in statistical analysis and PCA interpretation which is not appropriate for naive viewer.
  • The visualizations could be improved to make the legends and axis more clear to the viewer
